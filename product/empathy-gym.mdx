## How it works

The Empathy Gym creates immersive therapeutic conversations using AI client
personas that respond with realistic emotional dynamics. Every word you
type is analyzed in real time for empathy quality, bias signals, clinical
technique, and therapeutic alliance strength.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select a scenario">
    Choose from 410+ validated scenarios organized by therapeutic domain,
    difficulty level, and learning objective. The recommendation engine suggests
    scenarios based on your performance history and growth areas.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Engage in conversation">
    Interact with an AI client persona that responds with realistic emotional
    dynamics. The persona adapts its behavior based on your therapeutic approach
    — empathic responses build trust, while dismissive ones trigger realistic
    resistance.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receive real-time feedback">
    As you practice, the Feedback Panel provides continuous insight into your
    communication style, technique application, empathy score, and potential
    bias patterns.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and improve">
    After each session, access detailed analytics including session recording
    and playback, performance trends, and personalized recommendations for
    improvement.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Realistic emotional responses

Personas are powered by the [Emotional Intelligence Engine](/product/emotional-intelligence),
which models emotions using Plutchik's Wheel of Emotions across eight
primary dimensions:

```typescript
interface EmotionVector {
  joy: number // 0.0 – 1.0
  sadness: number // 0.0 – 1.0
  anger: number // 0.0 – 1.0
  fear: number // 0.0 – 1.0
  surprise: number // 0.0 – 1.0
  disgust: number // 0.0 – 1.0
  trust: number // 0.0 – 1.0
  anticipation: number // 0.0 – 1.0
}
```

Emotional state transitions are context-dependent. When a therapist validates
a persona's feelings, trust and joy increase while sadness decreases. When a
therapist is dismissive, anger and fear increase while trust drops. This
creates a realistic therapeutic dynamic where your approach directly shapes
the client's emotional trajectory.

<Tip>
  Watch the **Empathy Meter** during sessions — it provides a real-time visual
  gauge of therapeutic alliance strength based on the persona's emotional
  response to your interventions.
</Tip>

## Adaptive difficulty system

The Empathy Gym uses a five-level adaptive difficulty system that
progressively challenges your skills:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Level 1 — Beginner" icon="seedling">
    **Focus:** Foundational therapeutic skills

    Scenarios feature cooperative clients with straightforward presentations.
    Ideal for building confidence in active listening, reflective statements,
    and open-ended questions.

    *Example:* Initial session with a mildly depressed client following a job
    loss who is motivated to engage in therapy.

  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Level 2 — Intermediate" icon="leaf">
    **Focus:** Applied technique and mild complexity

    Scenarios introduce ambivalence, avoidance behaviors, and multi-layered
    presentations. Clients may resist certain interventions or test boundaries.

    *Example:* Motivational interviewing with a client ambivalent about changing
    alcohol use patterns, or CBT-I implementation for chronic insomnia.

  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Level 3 — Advanced" icon="tree">
    **Focus:** Complex clinical presentations

    Scenarios involve trauma processing, complicated grief, self-harm assessment,
    and personality disorder dynamics. Personas exhibit sophisticated defense
    mechanisms and emotional dysregulation.

    *Example:* Facilitating trauma processing with a combat veteran while
    monitoring for dissociation, or maintaining boundaries with a client showing
    borderline personality traits.

  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Level 4 — Expert" icon="mountain">
    **Focus:** Crisis intervention and high-stakes scenarios

    Scenarios include active suicidal ideation, acute psychosis, and multi-crisis
    presentations. Requires rapid assessment, safety planning, and clinical
    judgment under pressure.

    *Example:* Crisis intervention with a client reporting suicidal ideation and
    a specific plan following a relationship breakup, with a prior attempt
    history.

  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Level 5 — Nightmare Fuel" icon="fire">
    **Focus:** Edge cases and stress testing

    The most challenging scenarios in the library — designed to push even
    experienced clinicians to their limits. These include intersectional
    complexity, cultural landmines, ethical dilemmas, and rapid-escalation
    crises that test every skill simultaneously.

    *Example:* A client from a marginalized community presenting with comorbid
    PTSD and substance use who begins disclosing ongoing domestic violence
    mid-session while expressing passive suicidal ideation — with a cultural
    context that complicates standard safety planning protocols.

  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Note>
  The difficulty system is **adaptive** — it adjusts based on your performance.
  Consistently strong performance at one level triggers recommendations to
  advance. Struggles at a higher level generate targeted practice
  recommendations at an appropriate challenge point.
</Note>

## Session recording and playback

Every Empathy Gym session is automatically recorded for later review:

- **Full conversation transcript** with timestamped emotion analysis
- **Empathy score timeline** showing how your empathy quality changed
  throughout the session
- **Technique annotations** marking where you applied (or missed opportunities
  for) specific interventions
- **Bias event markers** highlighting any flagged moments for reflection
- **Supervisor sharing** — share recordings with clinical supervisors for
  feedback and competency review
